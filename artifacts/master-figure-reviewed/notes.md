## Figure concept

Proposal-scale overview with a top-row science story and a bottom-row program plan:

- left: the clinical data problem and the three imaging modalities;
- center: the Information Field Theory engine and the joint posterior target;
- right: validated outputs and the verification-to-clinical path;
- bottom: the four specific aims as the research program.

## Main content elements included

- central objective: reconstruct cardiac structure and hemodynamic fields from advanced imaging with quantified uncertainty;
- input modalities: PC-MRI, 4D Flow MRI, and Doppler Echo;
- methodological engine: physics-informed priors, measurement operators, representation/inference, and a posterior over fields and parameters;
- specific aims: inverse-problem formulation, time-evolving parameterization, scalable posterior inference, and verification/validation/demonstration;
- outputs and validation path: posterior fields with uncertainty, synthetic -> in vitro -> clinical evaluation.

## Important omissions and simplifications

- reduced the IFT subcomponents to short labels rather than detailed task-level bullet lists;
- omitted fine-grained task numbering inside each aim card to preserve proposal-scale readability;
- simplified outputs to the most proposal-salient quantities rather than listing every biomarker, comparison method, or broader-impact deliverable;
- used abstract vector icons instead of literal anatomical or scanner illustrations.

## Critique rounds

1. `Revise.` The first critic pass said the figure was semantically faithful but still too dense at full-width print scale, with small secondary text and a cramped seam above the aims row.
   Changes made: removed modality subtitles and the posterior subline, enlarged remaining secondary text, shortened panel copy, and lifted/shrank the posterior badge.

2. `Revise.` The second critic pass said the figure was cleaner but still overloaded in the chips and micro-labels.
   Changes made: dropped the left/right chip layers, simplified the engine boxes to short statements, reduced the right-panel detail stack, and further separated the top-row story from the aims row.

3. `Approved.` The critic judged the final version clear at a glance, proposal-ready, semantically aligned with the text, and light enough for print/PDF use.
